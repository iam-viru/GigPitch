# Upwork Cover Letter Proposal Guide

## Context & Purpose

This file teaches Claude how to help users write high-converting Upwork cover letter proposals. Use the checklist and power tips below as the framework when drafting, reviewing, or improving any Upwork proposal.

---

## The Cover Letter Proposal Checklist

### 1. Strong Opening
- The first **40–55 words** must immediately grab the client's attention.
- Ask internally: *"Am I showing expertise in their field, understanding of their need, and providing information they want to know?"*
- If the answer is no — rework the opening before proceeding.

### 2. Introduce Yourself
- Provide a **simple, clear description** of who you are.
- Focus on what makes you **uniquely qualified** for *their specific* need.
- Keep it concise — this is a supporting element, not the main focus.

### 3. How You Can Help Them
- Ask **insightful questions** that show you understand the project.
- Describe **how you will approach** the project specifically.
- Be genuinely helpful — remember: **clients care about their needs MORE than your background.**

### 4. Address Their Questions
- If the client asked questions in the job post, **answer them directly.**
- Pro tip: Combine this with Step 3 (How You Can Help) for a seamless, natural flow.

### 5. Call to Action (CTA)
- Ask them to **message you on Upwork.**
- Point them to your **Upwork Profile and/or Portfolio** so they can learn more.
- Make it **simple and clear** why they should contact you — this is the most important outcome.

### 6. List of Related Projects
- Include a **list of similar past projects** you've completed.
- For each project, provide:
  - A brief summary of what you did.
  - The **results or outcomes** achieved.
- Past experience builds **trust** — clients want someone reliable.

### 7. Include Upwork Portfolio Items
- Attach relevant **Upwork portfolio items** directly to the proposal.
- Upwork makes this easy — always use this feature.

### 8. Include Previous Upwork Jobs (If Related)
- In addition to portfolio items, attach **similar completed Upwork jobs.**
- This is a powerful proof point that you can deliver.

### 9. Proofread
- Run **spell check.**
- Check grammar using **Grammarly** before submitting.

## Power Tips (To Really Stand Out)

### Record a Loom Video
- Record a **short, personalized Loom video** and reference it in the cover letter.
- It does NOT need to be long — but it **must be specific to the client and their project.**
- A Loom video is one of the most effective ways to differentiate yourself.

### Add an Attachment
- Use the attachment option to showcase:
  - Projects **not in your Upwork Portfolio.**
  - Additional **sales copy** about who you are and how you work.

### Keep It Short
- Every checklist item can be covered in **one or two sentences.**
- Brevity signals **competence and clarity.**
- Keep proposals tight — give them a reason to contact you, not a reason to stop reading.

### Give Them a Reason to Contact You
- The **single most important goal** of any proposal is to get the client to message you.
- If you need more information to provide a better estimate — **say that explicitly.**
- Create a natural reason for dialogue.

---

## Writing Principles Claude Should Follow

| Principle | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Client-first framing | Always center the client's needs, not the freelancer's credentials |
| Specificity | Generic proposals lose — tailor every proposal to the specific job post |
| Brevity | Short proposals often outperform long ones; cut anything that doesn't serve the CTA |
| Trust building | Past results and social proof (portfolio, jobs) reduce client hesitation |
| Clear CTA | Every proposal must end with a specific, easy next step for the client |
